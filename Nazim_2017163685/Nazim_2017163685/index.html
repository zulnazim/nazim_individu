Father of Malaysia

From 1955 to 1957, the first Chief Minister of the Federation of Malaysia was Tunku Abdul Rahman Putra Al-Haj. He oversaw the transition of independence that ended on 31 August 1957. He dominated politics there as the first Prime Minister of Malaya for the next 13 years. In 1963, he effectively introduced into the state of Malaysia the Federation of Malaya, British North Borneo (renamed Sabah), Sarawak and Singapore.

Early Life

Tunku Abdul Rahman was born on 8 February 1903, at Istana Pelamin Palace in Alor Setar, Kedah, the seventh son and one of 45 children of Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah, the twenty-sixth ruler of the Kedah Sultanate. Tunku's mother was Che Manjalara (Nueang Nandanagara), a Thai, and the sixth wife of Sultan Abdul Hamid. She was the daughter of Luang Naraborirak (Kleb Nandanagara), a district officer in Thailand. He received early education at the Alor Setar Malay Primary School before furthering his studies at the government English school, Sultan Abdul Hamid College. He and his fellow siblings were later sent by his parents to Debsirin School in Bangkok. He returned home in 1915 to continue his education at the Penang Free School.
